Alberta. The proof.

Our flagship facility in Alberta is the operating heart of SkyFarms. Modular CEA units producing living lettuce, microgreens, and herbs for foodservice and retail partners — every day, in every season, at consistent specification.

This is where we proved the platform works — where the recipes are dialed in, the data layer matured, and the operating model that we'll deploy across Canada was stress-tested in real conditions. The crop quality consistency, the unit-economics math, the labour model, the supply-chain partnerships — all of it was built here, with hands on the system.

Operated in partnership with David & Alyssa Pfaeffli of Swiss Leaf Farms — container-farming pioneers operating in the prairies since 2018 — who bring hands-on expertise to daily facility operations and the practical experience that comes from years of real-world deployment.

The Squamish Valley facility sits on land we acquired in 2021 — a former hops farm in the Sea-to-Sky corridor between Vancouver and Whistler. It will be the second SkyFarms facility, and the first to run end-to-end on BC Hydro hydroelectric power.

The site is currently in repermitting under an updated building design we're calling "vertical farming 2.0" — the lessons from Alberta operationalised, the system optimised for BC's climate, energy mix, and water profile. We're working transparently with the District of Squamish through the permitting process.

The facility will produce for the Sea-to-Sky corridor first — Pemberton, Whistler, Squamish, Vancouver — a market that today imports nearly all of its leafy greens from California and Arizona at the end of a 1,500-mile cold chain. We can be at the back of the restaurant before the lettuce is even refrigerated.
