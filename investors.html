— Investment thesis

Seven reasons SkyFarms is not the next vertical-farming bonfire.

  1. Capital-markets DNA at the founder level.

    CEO & co-founder Daniel Cruz is a 20+ year capital-markets veteran. Co-founder of NASDAQ-traded Liquid Media Group. Combined with Charlie Brezer (President & Co-Founder; former President of Liquid Media Group Ltd.; 15+ years as a financial-industry seed investor), the founding team built SkyFarms with the public-markets endpoint in mind from day one.

  2. Farming-company DNA at the operating level.

    Alberta operations led by Container-farming pioneers David & Alyssa Pfaeffli, who have run Swiss Leaf Farms since 2018. Operations advised by Wayne Stigter, President & CEO of The Cucumber Man — one of Canada's leading produce companies. Global food-safety advised by Dr. Darin Detwiler (featured in Netflix's Poisoned). This is operating-ag depth at the senior team level, not stock photography.

  3. Modular containers, not monolithic warehouses.

    Plenty, Bowery, AppHarvest, and AeroFarms's first iteration all failed on the same structural problem: billion-dollar warehouses that required near-perfect utilisation to make the math work. Our model is structurally different. Each modular container is independently economic. Capex scales with offtake; yield risk is distributed; expansion is demand-paced.

  4. Operating today. Producing today.

    The Alberta facility is operational and producing for foodservice and retail partners. Engineering News rated it among the most efficient lettuce growers per square foot in the world. This is not a venture-backed projection — it's a working operation in its [CONFIRM] year.

  5. Crops where CEA actually wins.

    Living lettuce with the root attached — including commercial-scale living romaine, produced at scale by only a handful of companies globally. Living produce stays fresher weeks longer and is more nutritious than bagged lettuce that's travelled 3,000 miles dead. This is not commodity head-lettuce price competition with California.

  6. Renewable energy at the next facility.

    The Squamish Valley facility, currently in repermitting, will run on BC Hydro hydroelectric power. The energy cost-per-kilogram comparison that has bankrupted other vertical farms looks fundamentally different when your grid is renewable.

  7. Disciplined disclosure.

    Forward-looking-statement language consistent with securities norms in every press release. M&A advisory by Weiser Capital Group (Christos Livadas). Active discussions with Farm Credit Canada. Investor relations infrastructure already in place via our MyIR portal (Fairfax Partners). We are not bootstrapping into a public listing — we are being built to be one.

— Capital plan

Built to be listed.

Sea to Sky Farms Inc. is preparing for a public listing within the 2025–2026 window, contingent on market conditions. The capital structure ahead of listing comprises three components.

Equity: $2.5M CAD cash on balance sheet today. An equity round is in process — structure, size, use of proceeds, and runway funded available under NDA to qualified investors.

Debt: Active discussions with Farm Credit Canada on a working-capital and project-finance facility. FCC's mandate is purpose-aligned with Canadian CEA infrastructure.

Strategic: Partnership conversations advancing with strategic counterparties on offtake, distribution, and capital co-investment. [CONFIRM Northland disclosure with counterparty before publishing the partner's name.]
